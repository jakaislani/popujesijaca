10 Glen Powell Movies to Stream in 2024

August 2024 Â· 5 minute read

1

For Action Comedy Fans

Hit Man (2024)

With Hit Man, which is based on a true story, Glen Powell proves he's most definitely ready for leading man status. Hit Man follows the exploits of college professor Gary Johnson, whose soft-spoken persona is in direct contradiction with who he is after hours. When he's not teaching, Gary works for the New Orleans Police Department as a fake hitman, which is a pretty juicy premise.

Along with playing the film's lead character, Powell also co-wrote the flick with acclaimed director Richard Linklater, showing he has many hidden talents.

2

A Billon Dollar Box Office Hit

Top Gun: Maverick (2022)

Scoring a role in the long-awaited Top Gun sequel was an important moment for Glen Powell's career. In the movie, United States Navy Captain Pete "Maverick" Mitchell (Tom Cruise) is tasked with training a group of new recruits for an extremely dangerous mission.

Powell plays LT Jake "Hangman" Seresin, an over-confident pilot who is always at odds with his teammates. However, Hangman's brash exterior is gradually chipped away, and the audience starts to understand the man behind the bravado.

3

Your New Favorite Rom Com

Anyone But You (2023)

Following on from his success in Top Gun: Maverick, Glen Powell demonstrated his rom-com abilities as one of the leads in Anyone But You. Loosely based on William Shakespeare's Much Ado About Nothing, Anyone But You follows the love-hate relationship between Bea (Sydney Sweeney) and Ben (Powell). After a wonderful first date, some crossed wires turn the pair into enemies. But when they're thrown together at a wedding of mutual friends, Bea and Ben are forced to confront their true feelings for one another.

4

Cute Workplace Romantic Comedy

Set It Up (2018)

Netflix romantic comedy Set It Up brought Glen Powell to the general public's attention in a big way. The film focuses on Harper (Zoey Deutch) and Charlie (Powell) who work as assistants for some seriously fussy bosses. In an attempt to lighten their respective workloads, Harper and Charlie decide to set up their bosses (played by Taye Diggs and Lucy Liu), hoping romance will distract them from the office. But while they're busy meddling in other people's love lives, Charlie and Harper get much closer than they originally planned.

5

Nostalgic Sports Comedy

Everybody Wants Some!! (2016)

Prior to their collaboration on Hit Man, Glen Powell worked with director Richard Linklater on Everybody Wants Some!!, a sports comedy set in Texas in the 1980s. The film follows a group of college baseball players who enjoy their freedom a little too much, whether that's hosting house parties, smoking marijuana, or attempting to pick up women. Powell stars alongside Zoey Deutch, Tyler Hoechlin, and Wyatt Russel (Goldie Hawn and Kurt Russell's son) in the celebrated ensemble.

6

Nominated for 3 Oscars

Hidden Figures (2018)

Hidden Figures tells the incredible true story of the unsung Black women working at NASA during the Space Race. Based on Margot Lee Shetterly's book of the same name, Hidden Figures stars Taraji P. Henson, Octavia Spencer, and Janelle MonÃ¡e as three talented mathematicians.

While making significant contributions to the organization, the three women face prejudice, sexism, and racism in a heavily segregated industry. Glen Powell stars as astronaut John Glenn in the film, which was both a commercial and critical success.

7

Big Screen Action Flick

The Expendables 3 (2014)

Glen Powell got the chance to act opposite some action movie heavyweights when he joined the cast of The Expendables 3. In the third installment of the franchise, the Expendables have to stand up to former group member turned arms dealer Conrad Stonebanks (Mel Gibson) as he attempts to destroy them.

Powell portrays Thorn, a talented hacker and fighter, in the movie and gets to star alongside Hollywood royalty, including Sylvester Stallone, Arnold Schwarzenegger, Wesley Snipes, and Harrison Ford.

8

Romantic Historical Drama

The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society (2018)

Based on the novel by Mary Ann Shaffer and Annie Barrows, The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society is a historical drama about an unexpected book group. In 1946, author Juliet Ashton (Lily James) decides to write about the Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society after exchanging correspondence with one of its members. Once on the island, Juliet finds herself immersed in a close community of people, finding a sense of self she's never experienced before. Glen Powell portrays Juliet's American boyfriend, who is ready to get married before she absconds to Guernsey.

9

Biographical War Drama

Devotion (2022)

Devotion is a war drama based on the non-fiction book of the same name by Adam Makos. Set during the Korean War, the film tells the story of Jesse L. Brown, the very first Black aviator to serve in the United States Navy, and explores the working relationship he had with fighter pilot Tom Hudner (played by Glen Powell). Although the film wasn't a box office success, Powell was instrumental in getting the film made and served as a producer on the project.

10

Fast-Paced Crime Drama

Misconduct (2016)

Glen Powell joins an all-star cast including Josh Duhamel, Al Pacino, Anthony Hopkins, and Julia Stiles in thriller Misconduct. Duhamel plays Ben Cahill, a lawyer who is convinced by his ex-girlfriend to launch an ambitious and potentially lucrative class-action lawsuit against a huge pharmaceutical company. However, the more he delves into the case, the further he is steeped in a large-scale crime operation rife with death and destruction. Although Powell doesn't have the biggest role in the flick, fans will enjoy seeing him act opposite so many esteemed performers.
